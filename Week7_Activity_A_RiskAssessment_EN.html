Mission Brief

Your group is the political-risk team at an investment advisory. The board has just assigned you to assess the supply-chain political risk of one multinational. You have 50 minutes.

The board cares about: where in this MNC's supply chain are the single-points-of-failure? If the US–China rivalry escalates through 2027, where should the firm reposition between efficiency, resilience, and political alignment?

Steps

  1. 5 min — Read your assigned dossier carefully. Circle terms you don't know; assign a teammate to look them up.
  2. 10 min — Sketch a 5–8 layer supply chain tree (raw materials → end customer). Mark the dominant supplier (country/firm) at each node.
  3. 15 min — Fill the risk matrix. For each node, ask: substitutability? geo risk? impact if disrupted?
  4. 10 min — Tag at least 2 single-points-of-failure (toggle the SPOF flag). Write a 100-word executive summary.
  5. 10 min — Make a one-page infographic (paper or Canva). 3-minute group report.
